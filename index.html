SMC - Sharing Economy Concept

Dungeon Hero

Immerse yourself into retro-style gaming with Dungeon Hero, an epic world filled with classic RPG elements such as shining armors and ferocious monsters!

The project started off as an Arena grinder concept and ended up being a dungeon based role playing game.The role of the player is to control and aid the Torbjorn on his quest for revenge. The player has to decide which enemies to fight, decide when it fits best to rest, what armor to buy and to level up the character.

Mission

The mission of Particle Designs is to employ our knowledge and experience of marketing, design and coding to produce and enhance the visual identity of our clients, and create whatever digital solutions they require. We're happy when you're happy with what you've received, and we won't take a job if we can't see ourselves creating value for you.

Vision

The vision of Particle Designs is to continually learn and keep our existing knowledge up to date, so we will be able to assess what course will provide you, our client, with the most value we can give you. As we wrote above: If we aren't creating value for you, what's the point?

Values

Particle Designs has 3 core values. We are customer-focused: We aim to provide high-quality services that meet and surpass the expectations of our clients. The Team

Particle Designs is the natural development of two people - Ralf and John - working together over the course of several years. In late August 2014, we both started our Multimedia Design education at KEA - Copenhagens School of Design and Technology. From the very first day of that education, through various groups and tasks, we worked together. A shared passion for not just doing what was required, but for doing exceptional work, was what drove us forward and, 2 years down the line, lead to us to the creation of Particle Designs.

SMC

Sharing Economy Concept

The concept of SMC springs from the desire both to be more helpful and more personally engaging than many service­style guided tours often are. The idea is to allow users to cater to each other based on more exact, specified needs. Whether those needs are to be shown around a new city to get to know where basic shopping possibilities are, or go into far more specific interests.

How To Be A Spy For An Afternoon

Writing and designing content for the web.

The "How to be a spy for an afternoon" article came about as one article of several in an e-zine that we produced. Thematically we were bound to urban expression and living under the overall umbrella theme of art. The research involved in finding interesting subcultures and off the radar experiences to write about was a very interesting experience for us, and on where we're proud of the product that was made.

With writing, art and graphical design experience under our belts, this project helped expand our horizons more than it gave us new competences. The project was done at the Copenhagen School of Design and Technology.

Dungeon Hero

Retro-style Role Playing Game

Dungeon Hero was not the first game we made with Javascript, JQuery and the CreateJS framework. Rather, it represented several months of on and off testing and development of game systems (health, gold, levels and their design, combat, etc) and how game run in general.

While our personal love of the RPG genre influenced the choice of game that was made in this case, the lessons learned apply to all sorts of games. In the process of development we learned lessons that can be applied to 2-player games, quizzes, and a variety of other interesting web-apps.
